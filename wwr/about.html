White Water Rafting

The core goal of whitewater rafting is to navigate the river safely and successfully, using teamwork and skill to conquer the challenging rapids. Beyond navigation, the goal is to provide an exhilarating, adrenaline-filled recreational experience while connecting participants with the natural beauty of the river environment.

History

The history of whitewater rafting began with Native American river travel and the 19th-century explorations of pioneers like John Wesley Powell, who mapped the Grand Canyon using sturdy wooden boats. The sport's modern foundation was laid in the 1940s and 1950s when World War II surplus inflatable rubber rafts became available for recreational use. Clyde Smith ran one of the first commercial trips in 1940. The activity rapidly grew in the 1960s and 1970s as technology advanced to create durable, maneuverable, self-bailing rafts, transforming necessary river navigation into a widely popular commercial adventure sport governed by safety standards like the International Scale of River Difficulty.
